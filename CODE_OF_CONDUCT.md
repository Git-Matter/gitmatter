# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

gitmatter should be a respectful, professional, and useful place to collaborate. Technical
disagreement is welcome. Harassment, intimidation, personal attacks, and bad-faith participation
are not.

Examples of behavior that supports this standard include:

- giving specific, constructive feedback
- disagreeing with ideas without attacking people
- respecting different backgrounds, identities, experiences, and levels of expertise
- accepting correction and taking responsibility for mistakes
- protecting private, confidential, and security-sensitive information
- focusing on what is best for users and the wider community

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, threats, stalking, or deliberate intimidation
- discriminatory, sexualized, insulting, or demeaning language or imagery
- trolling, sustained disruption, or repeated bad-faith arguments
- publishing another person's private information without permission
- sharing confidential legal documents, credentials, provider keys, security reports, or other
  sensitive data
- retaliation against someone who raises a concern in good faith
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This code applies in repository activity and project spaces, including issues, pull requests,
discussions, reviews, and other official communication. It also applies when someone publicly
represents gitmatter or its community.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to [contact@gitmatter.com](mailto:contact@gitmatter.com). Include
the relevant links, context, and any supporting material you can safely share.

Do not include secrets, private legal documents, or unrelated personal information. Reports will
be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible, but information may be shared when needed to
investigate or respond fairly.

Security vulnerabilities should instead follow the private reporting process in
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code. They may edit or remove
contributions and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants whose behavior
they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

When deciding how to respond, maintainers will consider the context, seriousness, impact, and
pattern of behavior. They will seek a proportionate outcome and avoid conflicts of interest where
reasonably possible.

Good-faith reports will not result in retaliation. Knowingly false or misleading reports may
themselves violate this code.
